Let me just say this upfront. If you're military or a military family looking to buy in Colorado, VA loans are hands down the best assumable mortgages on the market right now. Not even close.

Here's the thing. Most people don't know VA loans are assumable at all. And the ones who do? They're not fully grasping how powerful that is. So let me walk you through why this matters so much.

## VA Loans Are Assumable. Most Mortgages Aren't.

First, the basic fact. A VA loan can be assumed by someone else. That means if you own a home with a VA mortgage, the next buyer can take over your loan. They inherit your rate, your terms, your payment. Everything.

Conventional loans? Not assumable. FHA loans? Not assumable. USDA loans? Not assumable.

VA loans stand alone.

Now, why does that matter? Because we're in a weird interest rate environment. Rates have climbed. A lot. So when someone with a VA loan locked in at 2.5% or 3% puts their home on the market, that assumability becomes a massive selling advantage. Buyers see that rate and suddenly the entire deal looks different.

Let me show you the math on this.

## The Actual Dollar Impact

Let's say you're a military family in Colorado Springs. You bought your home five years ago with a VA loan at 2.8%. You financed $350,000. Your payment was around $1,470 a month (principal and interest only).

Today's rates are hovering around 6.8% for a conventional loan. If a buyer wants to purchase your home for the same price but needs a new mortgage, their payment jumps to $2,340 a month. That's an extra $870 every single month.

Over 30 years, that's $313,200 more out of their pocket.

But if they assume your VA loan? They step into that 2.8% rate. They take your $1,470 payment. Suddenly, your home becomes the only viable option in the neighborhood at that price point.

That's not just a selling advantage. That's a competitive moat.

## Why VA Loans Are Different

The VA doesn't care if the buyer is military or civilian. They don't care if the buyer has a VA loan elsewhere. The loan can be assumed by literally anyone who qualifies to assume it.

That's unusual. Most loan products are locked tight. The original borrower is stuck. The next buyer has to requalify, refinance, start from scratch.

Not with VA.

The assumption process still requires the buyer to qualify. The lender will review their credit, their income, their debt-to-income ratio. But the rate doesn't change. The term doesn't change. All they're doing is stepping into your shoes on that mortgage.

Here's something else that's wild. If the home sells for less than the loan balance, the seller is protected. The buyer assumes the full loan amount even though the home is worth less. That's called an underwater assumption, and it's part of why VA loans are built for military families. The VA has your back.

Those homes are sitting on gold. Not gold that's easy to spend, but gold nonetheless.

And here's the kicker. You can use your VA eligibility on a future purchase. You don't lose it when you assume. That's not true with some loan types. With VA, you can assume someone else's loan and still have your full benefit intact for the next move.

## What About Non-Military Buyers?

Okay, so you're not military. Can you still assume a VA loan in Colorado?

Yes. 100%. The VA doesn't care about your service status. If the seller agrees and the lender approves, you can assume it.

The only catch? You can't get the VA's loan guarantee to back that assumption. So the lender is taking more risk. Some lenders won't do it. Some will, but they'll add extra scrutiny. If you've got solid credit and income, most major lenders will work with you.

The numbers still make sense. You're walking into a 3% rate when new loans are 6.8%. Even with extra scrutiny, that math wins.
