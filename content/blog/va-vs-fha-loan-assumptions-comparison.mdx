When you're buying a home with an assumable loan in Colorado, you may have a choice between VA and FHA assumptions. Both programs let you take over the seller's mortgage, but they work differently. Here's what every Colorado buyer should know.

## Key Differences Between VA and FHA Assumptions

VA loans and FHA loans are designed for different borrower profiles. VA loans are only for military members, veterans, and their surviving spouses. FHA loans are available to civilian buyers with lower down payments and credit scores.

When you assume a VA loan, you're taking over the seller's mortgage under Veterans Affairs terms. With an FHA assumption, you take over an FHA-insured loan, which carries mortgage insurance.

## Cost Comparison: VA vs FHA

This is where the math gets important. VA assumptions typically cost less than FHA assumptions because VA loans don't require mortgage insurance.

With an FHA assumption, you'll typically pay:
- Upfront mortgage insurance premium (UFMIP): 1.75% of the loan amount
- Annual mortgage insurance premium (MIP): 0.55% of the remaining balance each year

That's real money. On a $400,000 FHA loan, the UFMIP alone is $7,000. The annual MIP adds hundreds to your monthly payment.

VA assumptions don't have insurance. The VA guarantees the lender's risk, not the borrower's. Your VA assumption payment is your actual payment, with no insurance tacked on.

## Timeline and Approval

VA assumptions typically take 30 to 45 days, depending on the lender's process. The VA must verify your eligibility, the seller must agree, and the lender must approve your financial profile.

FHA assumptions follow a similar timeline but sometimes take longer because the FHA insurer must sign off. You're also required to get an appraisal and full underwriting approval.

## Credit and Income Requirements

VA loans have no credit score minimum if you have 0% down. Most VA lenders want a 620+ credit score for assumptions, but it's flexible.

FHA assumptions require a minimum 500 credit score, though 620+ is preferred. You need documented income, stable employment history, and a debt-to-income ratio under 43%.

## Which Should You Choose?

If you're eligible for a VA assumption, it's usually your cheapest option. No mortgage insurance, lower interest rates, and faster approval. If you're not military-connected, FHA assumptions beat conventional refinances for most Colorado buyers with lower credit or limited down payments.

The best choice depends on your eligibility and the specific loan you're assuming. Get quotes from both programs and compare your actual monthly payment, not just the interest rate.
