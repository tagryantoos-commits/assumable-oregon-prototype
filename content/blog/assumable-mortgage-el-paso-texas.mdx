# Assumable Mortgage El Paso, Texas

El Paso doesn't get enough credit as an assumable mortgage market.

It should.

Fort Bliss is one of the largest Army installations in the country. 1st Armored Division, Army Air and Missile Defense Command, dozens of smaller units. Tens of thousands of soldiers cycle through on 2-4 year orders. When they leave, a lot of them leave behind VA loans originated at 2019-2022 rates.

The public loan data confirms what anyone who's looked at El Paso knows: there are tens of thousands of VA and FHA loans in El Paso County with rates under 5%. A lot of them are under 3.5%. These are loans that were locked in when rates were at generational lows, and they're sitting inside homes that are selling right now.

The question is whether buyers know to look for them.

## Why El Paso Is Different

Most buyers in El Paso are shopping conventionally. They're calling lenders, getting pre-approved at 6.25%, and competing for homes the same way everyone else is.

A smaller group knows what Fort Bliss's rotation cycle actually means for the market: a constant, predictable supply of assumable VA loans at rates that are simply not available anywhere else.

El Paso's median home price is around $220,000-$240,000. That price point, combined with the deep VA loan inventory, creates one of the best assumable mortgage opportunities in Texas.

## The Numbers

Let's run the math on a $225,000 home.

**Assumable at 3.0%:**
- Monthly payment (P&I): $949
- Total interest over 30 years: $111,640

**Conventional at 6.25% on the same home:**
- Monthly payment (P&I): $1,385
- Total interest over 30 years: $273,800

That's $436 per month in savings. $162,160 over 30 years.

El Paso is an affordable market. That savings figure represents a massive percentage of your monthly budget compared to higher-priced metros. For a lot of buyers here, $436 a month is the difference between a comfortable monthly budget and a tight one.

## The Fort Bliss Effect

Fort Bliss generates a specific kind of assumable inventory: primarily VA loans, concentrated in the northeast and east El Paso neighborhoods closest to the installation. Areas like Horizon City, El Paso East, and the neighborhoods feeding into Montana Avenue and Gateway have deep military homeowner concentrations.

But VA loans are spread across the whole city. Soldiers live in central El Paso, the westside, the Upper Valley. The inventory isn't limited to one neighborhood.

What's interesting about Fort Bliss specifically is that it houses a lot of mid-to-senior enlisted soldiers. E-5 through E-7 is the typical homeowner range in military markets, and in El Paso that means home prices between $180,000 and $280,000, which keeps the equity gap manageable for most buyers.

## Who Can Assume

I'll say it again because it still surprises people: you don't have to be a veteran.

Non-veterans can legally assume VA loans. The seller's entitlement stays with the property, which means the vet can't use it again until you pay off or refinance. Some sellers won't do it because of the entitlement issue. Many will, especially if they're under orders and just need to close.

Veterans assuming VA loans: the cleanest path. Full entitlement restored to the seller, no complications, and you step into a rate the market hasn't seen in years.

El Paso has a strong military community culture. Word about assumable mortgages spreads. More sellers here understand what an assumption is than in non-military markets, which makes conversations easier.

## The Equity Gap Math in El Paso

El Paso property values have appreciated, but not dramatically compared to coastal markets. That's actually an advantage for assumption buyers.

A home purchased in 2021 for $200,000 might list today at $240,000. The VA loan balance might be $180,000. Your equity gap: $60,000.

Options:
- Bring $60,000 cash
- Put $20,000-$30,000 down and finance the gap with a second mortgage
- Negotiate purchase price and reduce the gap

Even a second mortgage scenario holds up financially. Take $180,000 at 2.875% and $45,000 at 7.5%. Blended monthly payment: around $1,290. A full conventional at 6.25% on $225,000: $1,385. You're still ahead, you've put less cash down, and you have the low-rate loan locked in for decades.

Run your own numbers. The math almost always favors the assumption when the gap is under $80,000 in a $220,000-$240,000 price range.

## FHA Loans in El Paso

El Paso isn't just a VA market. It's also a strong FHA market.

A lot of El Paso buyers in 2019-2022 used FHA loans because they had lower credit scores, less than 20% down, or didn't have VA eligibility. FHA loans are also assumable. And they're available to anyone who qualifies, veteran or not.

The advantage of FHA assumptions: the pool of sellers is broader. You're not limited to veteran-owned properties. Any FHA borrower who bought at the right time is a potential seller with an assumable loan.

FHA assumption process is similar to VA: servicer approval, income and credit check, 45-75 day timeline. The main difference is that FHA loans require the seller to be released from liability at closing, which is standard and handled by a good assumption processor.

## Doing It Right in Texas

Texas is a deed-of-trust state with a fast, clean title process. Assumptions work smoothly here when everyone knows the process.

The flow:

1. Identify assumable properties with loans from the 2019-2022 window
2. Make an offer with an assumption contingency
3. Submit to the servicer with income and credit documentation
4. Servicer reviews, issues approval
5. Close and take over the loan

Some Texas servicers are faster than others. USAA handles a lot of military loans and has gotten better at assumptions. But no matter the servicer, 45-75 days is the realistic window. Plan for it. Factor it into your offer terms.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are assumable mortgages available outside Colorado?
Yes. Any property with an existing FHA, VA, or USDA loan is potentially assumable, regardless of state. The process is the same nationwide, though servicer responsiveness varies.

### Which states have the most assumable mortgage inventory?
States with high military populations (Texas, Virginia, North Carolina, Georgia, Washington, Florida) and states with high FHA loan usage tend to have the most assumable inventory. Colorado also ranks high due to its military bases.

### How do I find assumable homes in other states?
Look for listings that mention "assumable" in MLS remarks. Ask your local agent to filter for FHA and VA sales from 2019-2022. Working with a specialist who tracks assumable inventory is the most reliable approach.

### Is the assumption process different in other states?
The federal loan rules are the same nationwide (FHA, VA, USDA are all assumable). State-specific differences involve title, recording, and closing processes, but the mortgage assumption mechanics are identical.

### Can I assume a mortgage remotely in another state?
Yes. Much of the assumption application process can be done remotely. Closing typically requires either physical presence or a power of attorney arrangement.
