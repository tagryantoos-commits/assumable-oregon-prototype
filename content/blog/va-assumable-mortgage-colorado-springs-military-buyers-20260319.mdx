If you're military or a military spouse looking to buy in Colorado Springs, I want to walk you through something that's genuinely changing the game for our veteran community. VA assumable mortgages. And I'm not exaggerating when I say some of you are sitting on the ability to save $200K, $300K, even more over the life of a loan.

Here's the thing. A lot of people don't know this exists. Even some lenders don't fully understand it. But if the seller has a VA loan with a rate locked in below today's market rate, you can assume that loan. Keep their rate. Skip a ton of closing costs. Move in with a way lower payment.

Let me break down why this matters so much right now in Colorado Springs.

## The Rate Reality Right Now

Current mortgage rates are sitting somewhere in the high 6s to low 7s for a conventional 30 year loan. Let's say 6.75%.

Now, a lot of VA loans out there were written when rates were 2.5%, 3%, even 4%. Those rates are still on those mortgages. And if you can assume one of those loans, you're not getting a new rate. You're keeping their old rate.

Think about that for a second. A seller closes out. You step in and take over their 3% loan instead of getting a brand new one at 6.75%. That's a 3.75 percentage point difference.

On a $400K loan, that's the difference between a $2,686 monthly payment and a $1,520 monthly payment. That's $1,166 a month. That's $13,992 a year. Over 30 years, we're talking about savings north of $400K.

And here's the part that gets me. You don't even need perfect credit to assume a VA loan. You just need to qualify. You just need to be able to carry the debt.

## Why Colorado Springs Specifically

Colorado Springs has a massive military population. Fort Carson, Peterson Space Force Base, Schriever Space Force Base. This isn't a city with a handful of military families. This is a military town. That means there are a lot of VA loans in circulation here. A lot of potential assumptions sitting on the market.

Plus, the Colorado Springs market has been moving. We're not in a buyer's market anymore, but we're not in a full seller's market either. There's actually room to negotiate. And when you can show a seller "Hey, my VA assumption closes faster, requires less paperwork, actually works better for both of us," it opens doors.

Sellers get this too. They like assumptions because the VA handles a lot of the underwriting. The process is cleaner. Fewer contingencies blow up deals.

## The Math on Closing Costs

Here's another part people miss. When you assume a loan, you're not paying a new loan origination fee. You're not paying all the upfront junk fees a lender would normally hit you with. The VA has limits on what can be charged for an assumption. We're talking $500 to maybe $2,000 in total fees, not $6,000 to $12,000.

If you're assuming a $400K loan instead of getting a new one, you're already ahead by $4,000 to $10,000 before you even turn the key.

## Who Can Assume a VA Loan

This is important. You don't have to be military to assume a VA loan. You can be a civilian. A spouse. Anyone. You just need to qualify and get approval from the VA.

Now, if you're not military, the seller's VA entitlement won't transfer to you. That matters for their future use. But for you as the buyer, you get the low rate. You get the payment. You get the deal.

If you ARE military or a surviving spouse, you can potentially restore the seller's entitlement. This gets a little technical, but it means they can use their VA benefit again in the future. The VA actually encourages military buyers to assume VA loans for exactly this reason.

## The Catch

I want to be straight with you. Not every VA loan can be assumed. Some have restrictions. Some sellers aren't open to it. And the seller does have to give permission and work with you on it. It's not something you can do without them.

Also, you need to find these properties first. Assumptions don't advertise themselves. The MLS doesn't have a filter that says "VA assumable at 2.8%." You have to know what you're looking for. You have to have someone who understands the market and knows where these deals are hiding.
