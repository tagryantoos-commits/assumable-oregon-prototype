# Assumable Mortgage Texas: The Complete Guide

Texas is the most active assumable mortgage state in the country outside California — and most buyers shopping here have never heard of this option.

The reason is straightforward: no state concentrates more active-duty military, VA loan originations, and civilian FHA buying in one place than Texas. Fort Cavazos is the largest Army installation in the world by active-duty population. Joint Base San Antonio is one of the largest multi-branch military complexes anywhere. Fort Bliss anchors the El Paso military market. The combined VA and FHA loan volume these installations generated during the 2019-2022 rate window was staggering — and those loans are now cycling through the market as soldiers PCS, retire, and relocate.

For a buyer in Texas today, an assumable mortgage can mean $800 to $1,400 less per month on the same home. Over a 30-year loan, that is $290,000 to $500,000 in total interest savings. The equity gap is real and must be funded. The process takes longer than a conventional purchase. But the financial advantage is documented, available right now, and concentrated in some of the most affordable markets in the country.

This guide covers every major Texas assumable market — with payment math, equity gap data, and what the process actually looks like on the ground.

## Why Texas Has So Many Assumable Loans

### The Military Engine

Texas hosts more active-duty military personnel than all but one other state. The numbers:

- **140,000+ active-duty military** stationed at Texas installations
- **1.6 million veterans** living in Texas — the second-largest veteran population in the US
- **VA loan originations:** Texas consistently ranks in the top two states nationally for VA loan volume each year
- **Fort Cavazos alone** (Killeen) generates more VA loans per year than most states produce in total

These servicemembers bought heavily during 2019-2022 when VA rates were at generational lows. 30-year VA loans locked at 2.25%-3.50% during that window are now assumable. When soldiers receive PCS orders, they list their homes — and the assuming buyer steps into the loan.

### The Civilian FHA Wave

Texas real estate markets outside the military corridor were also extremely active during 2020-2022. Houston, Dallas-Fort Worth, and Austin saw massive first-time buyer and move-up buyer FHA purchase volume as millennials chased affordable rates. Those buyers locked FHA loans at sub-3.5% on homes that have since appreciated 20-35%.

The result: FHA assumable inventory is scattered across every major Texas metro, not just military markets. Houston's outer suburbs, DFW's Tarrant County and Kaufman County, San Antonio's Bexar County, and Austin's Williamson County all have substantial FHA loan pools from the 2020-2022 buying period.

## Who Can Assume a Mortgage in Texas

### FHA Assumptions: Open to All Buyers

Any buyer who qualifies on credit and income can assume an FHA loan in Texas — military service is not required. The buyer must intend to occupy the home as a primary residence in most cases. Credit score minimums vary by servicer: typically 580-620, with better pricing at 640+. Debt-to-income ratios follow standard FHA guidelines (43-45% DTI with compensating factors).

FHA assumptions are most common in Houston's outer suburbs, DFW's Tarrant and Collin County markets, San Antonio's civilian neighborhoods, and Austin's ring suburbs. Any buyer shopping these markets should be asking about FHA assumption eligibility on every listing with a 2019-2022 origination date.

### VA Assumptions: The Veteran Question (Civilians Qualify Too)

The most common misconception about VA loans is that only veterans can assume them. **That is wrong.** Civilians can assume VA loans. The VA does not restrict assumption by military status — the servicer qualifies the assuming buyer on credit and income regardless of whether they have ever served.

The consideration for the seller: when a non-veteran assumes a VA loan, the veteran seller's VA entitlement stays attached to that loan until it is paid off or refinanced. The seller cannot use VA benefits on their next purchase until that entitlement is restored — unless the assuming buyer is also a veteran and can complete an **entitlement substitution**.

**Entitlement substitution** is the cleanest structure when both parties are veterans. The buyer's VA entitlement substitutes in, the seller's entitlement is immediately restored, and both parties can use VA benefits on future purchases. In Texas's military-dense markets — Killeen, San Antonio, El Paso — this structure is common.

For non-veteran buyers assuming VA loans: you still get the sub-3.5% rate, but the seller should understand the entitlement implication before agreeing to the transaction. Some veterans will prefer entitlement substitution if they plan to buy another home soon; others will not care if they are moving off base housing permanently.

[Full guide: Can non-veterans assume VA loans?](/blog/can-non-veterans-assume-va-loans)

### Income and Credit Thresholds

Texas incomes tend to be moderate relative to coastal markets, which works in buyers' favor for qualification. The debt-to-income ratio calculation uses the assumed payment (which is lower than a new loan payment), giving buyers more room to qualify. A buyer who would be at the edge of qualification for a 6.75% loan will typically qualify more comfortably for the assumed 2.875% payment on the same home.

Credit requirements: VA assumptions typically require 580+ (servicer-dependent, many prefer 620+). FHA assumptions track FHA guidelines (580+ with 3.5% down, 500-579 with 10% down). Most buyers in Texas's target markets — workforce housing, military families, first-time buyers — can clear these thresholds with proper preparation.

## The Texas Assumption Process

The process in Texas follows the national framework with servicer-specific timelines:

1. **Offer with assumption language.** Your contract should identify the loan type, estimated balance, origination rate, and buyer's intent to assume. Texas REALTORS contract forms accommodate this — work with an agent who understands the specific language.

2. **Identify the servicer.** The seller's loan servicer handles the assumption, not the original lender. Common servicers in Texas military markets include Mr. Cooper, Pennymac, NewRez/Shellpoint, Freedom Mortgage, PHH Mortgage, and LoanCare. Call the servicer's assumption department early — timelines vary widely.

3. **Submit the assumption package.** Credit authorization, income documentation (last two years W-2s, tax returns, 30 days pay stubs), asset statements. Treat it as a full loan application.

4. **Underwriting.** Servicer underwrites the assuming buyer. This is the longest phase. Budget 30-60 days. Some servicers (Mr. Cooper, Pennymac) have established assumption departments and move faster. Others are slower. Ask about current timelines upfront.

5. **Coordinate gap financing simultaneously.** If you are not paying the equity gap in cash, your gap lender needs to be ready to close on the same day as the assumption approval. Don't wait for servicer approval before engaging your gap lender. [Current gap loan lenders](/blog/assumable-mortgage-gap-loan-lenders-2026)

6. **Close.** Once the servicer approves the assumption, closing follows within a week to ten days. You bring the gap funds, closing costs (typically $2,500-$4,500 in Texas), and exit with a sub-3.5% loan.

**Total timeline: 45-90 days** from accepted offer to close. Killeen and San Antonio assumptions tend to close faster than Austin or Houston due to agent and servicer familiarity. Budget 75 days as a working number.

## Texas-Specific Considerations

**PCS Season Is Year-Round in Texas.** Killeen, San Antonio, and El Paso generate PCS moves continuously — not just in the spring-summer peak. Unlike states with one or two installations, Texas's multi-installation concentration means assumable inventory comes to market in every month of the year. There is no wrong time to search.

**Texas Has No State Income Tax.** This has implications for the assumption math: buyers keeping $600-$1,000/month more in their pocket by assuming instead of financing at market rates effectively capture a larger real benefit than the nominal savings suggest, because that money is not subject to state tax in Texas.

**Killeen and El Paso Are Buyer-Competitive Markets.** In 2020-2022, these markets saw multiple-offer scenarios. The assumption market is different — fewer buyers understand the process, which means assumption-eligible listings see less competition than conventional listings. This is an advantage for prepared buyers.

**Austin's Gap Financing Requirements Are Substantial.** Austin equity gaps of $150,000-$250,000 require serious gap financing or significant cash reserves. Not every gap loan lender operates at this scale. If you are shopping Austin assumables, identify your gap financing source before making offers.

**Texas Title Companies and Assumptions.** Texas uses title companies for closings rather than attorneys. Most Texas title companies have processed VA and FHA assumptions before, but experience varies. Ask your title company how many assumptions they have closed in the last 12 months. Experience matters in navigating the coordination between servicer approval and simultaneous gap loan closing.

**Veteran Population and Market Awareness.** Texas has 1.6 million veterans. Awareness of the assumable opportunity is growing in military communities — but most civilian buyers in Houston, Austin, and DFW still have not heard of it. Being one of the few informed buyers in a large market is a meaningful competitive advantage.

## Texas Assumable Mortgage FAQ

**Can a civilian buyer assume a VA loan from a Fort Cavazos soldier?**

Yes. Civilian buyers can assume VA loans without military service. The servicer qualifies you on credit and income. The seller's VA entitlement situation is a separate consideration — if the seller plans to buy again with VA benefits, they may prefer a veteran buyer for entitlement substitution. But the assumption itself is available to you regardless.

**How much cash do I need to assume a home in Killeen or El Paso?**

Killeen and El Paso have the most accessible entry points for assumption buyers in Texas. In Killeen, buyers with $50,000-$75,000 in cash or financing can often assume directly without gap loans. In El Paso, $45,000-$70,000 frequently covers the equity gap. Closing costs add $2,500-$4,000. Budget $55,000-$80,000 total in these markets for a cash-to-close target range.

**How does the assumption process work if I'm in another state and buying in Texas?**

Military buyers PCSing to Texas can initiate assumption transactions remotely. Work with a buyer's agent in the destination market who understands assumption transactions. Electronic signatures are standard on Texas contracts. The physical close can be done via mail-away or power of attorney in most cases. The assumption itself is processed directly with the servicer regardless of your current location.

**Will an assumable mortgage give me an edge in a competitive Texas market?**

In military markets (Killeen, San Antonio, El Paso), assumptions are less competitive than conventional listings because fewer buyers understand the process. Sellers in these markets often prefer qualified assumption buyers because the deal is more likely to close — servicer approval is predictable in a way that conventional loan approvals sometimes are not. In Austin and Houston, conventional buyers dominate and assumption-eligible listings receive less focused attention. You are not competing in a hot pool.

**What credit score do I need for a VA loan assumption in Texas?**

Servicer minimums vary, but most require 580-620 for VA assumptions. Higher scores (640+) open better terms on any gap financing you need. FHA assumptions typically follow FHA minimums: 580 with standard underwriting. If your credit is below these thresholds, a 60-90 day credit improvement plan often brings you to qualification.
