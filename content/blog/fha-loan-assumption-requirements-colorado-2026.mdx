# FHA Loan Assumption Requirements in Colorado: 2026 Guide

You found the house. The price is right. The seller has an FHA loan with a rate locked in five years ago. Now you're wondering: can you just take over their mortgage?

The answer is yes. But there are rules. And they're specific. This guide walks you through what Colorado buyers need to know about FHA loan assumptions in 2026.

## Why FHA Assumptions Matter Right Now

Most FHA loans are assumable. That's the good news. It means you can skip the whole process of getting a new loan approval, new appraisal, and the waiting game. You step into the seller's loan terms.

The savings come fast. If the seller locked in a 3.2% rate two years ago and the market is at 6.5%, assuming their loan means you pay that 3.2% rate. Over a 30-year mortgage on a $350,000 home, that difference is roughly $580 per month in your pocket.

But "assumable" doesn't mean automatic. The lender still needs to approve you. And the rules changed slightly in 2026.

## Who Can Assume an FHA Loan in Colorado

**You need to qualify.** This is the part people miss. Just because the loan is assumable doesn't mean you automatically get it.

The lender will run a credit check, verify your income, and look at your debt-to-income ratio (DTI). They're not starting from scratch like a new loan application, but they're definitely looking at whether you can handle the payment.

**Credit score requirement:** Most lenders want 580 or higher for an FHA assumption. Some want 620. It depends on the lender. You'll find this varies more in 2026 than it did five years ago. Ask the seller's lender early in the process. This saves time.

**Income verification:** You'll provide pay stubs, tax returns, and possibly bank statements. The lender wants to see that your income covers the assumed mortgage payment, property taxes, insurance, HOA fees (if applicable), and any other debt you're carrying.

**Debt-to-income ratio:** This is the number that kills deals. Your total monthly debt payments (including the new mortgage payment) divided by your gross monthly income. For FHA assumptions, most lenders want this at 50% or lower. Some go to 55% if your credit is strong and reserves are solid.

If you're close but not quite there, this is where a co-signer sometimes helps.

**Occupancy requirement:** This one's important. You must intend to occupy the property as your primary residence. FHA doesn't allow assumptions on investment properties or vacation homes. That's federal rule, not local.

## The Approval Process: Step by Step

Assuming an FHA loan isn't complicated, but it has to happen in the right order.

**Step 1: Get the loan details from the seller.**
Ask your agent to request the current loan information. You need the lender's name, loan number, current balance, remaining term, and the interest rate. You also need to know the current principal and interest payment (not including taxes and insurance).

**Step 2: Contact the lender directly.**
Call the servicer of the loan and ask about their assumption requirements. Every lender has slightly different documentation needs. Some want everything upfront. Others move it slowly. Get their timeline.

**Step 3: Submit assumption application and docs.**
You'll fill out an assumption application. The lender will ask for credit authorization, financial statements, and tax returns. This typically takes two weeks.

**Step 4: Appraisal (sometimes).**
If the home's current value is significantly different from the loan balance, the lender might order a new appraisal. This is lender-specific. Budget 10 days if they do.

**Step 5: Approval and assumption agreement.**
Once approved, you'll receive an assumption agreement to sign. This replaces the original promissory note with one bearing your name. Title company handles this as part of closing.

The whole process typically runs 30 to 45 days. It's faster than a new loan if you've got your documents ready.

## Costs You'll Pay

This is where assumptions get interesting. You're not starting a brand new loan, so some closing costs disappear. But others don't.

**You'll pay:**
- Assumption fee (lender charges this, typically $500 to $1,500)
- Title search and insurance (required, usually $800 to $1,200)
- Recording fees and attorney fees ($300 to $500)
- Home inspection and appraisal (if ordered, $400 to $800)

**You won't pay:**
- Loan origination fee (this is huge. Usually 0.5% to 1% of the loan amount)
- Underwriting fees (usually $400 to $600)
- Processing fees
- Credit check fees (the lender typically covers this for assumptions)

Total out of pocket usually falls between $2,000 and $4,000. Compare that to closing costs on a new mortgage, which run 2% to 5% of the purchase price.

## Remaining Loan Balance and Your Down Payment

Here's something that trips people up. The seller's remaining loan balance isn't necessarily the purchase price.

Let's say the house is appraised at $400,000. The seller's FHA loan still has $280,000 left. You're not buying a $280,000 house. You're buying a $400,000 house.

The math works like this:
- Purchase price: $400,000
- Assumed loan balance: $280,000
- Your down payment needed: $120,000

You still need to bring cash to the table for the difference. The advantage is that you're getting the seller's low rate. The disadvantage is your down payment is based on current market value, not the outstanding loan balance.

If you're planning to assume a loan, factor this into your offer. Some sellers with older FHA loans have significant equity built up, and your down payment will reflect that.

## FHA Assumptions vs. New Loans in Colorado

When does an assumption make sense?

**Assume if:**
- The seller's rate is 1.5% or more below market rate
- You have solid income and credit (580+ score, 45% or lower DTI)
- The remaining loan term works for you (you don't need a brand new 30-year loan)
- Your down payment is ready
- Closing timeline is flexible (4 to 6 weeks)

**Get a new loan if:**
- The seller's rate is within 0.5% of market
- Your credit or income doesn't qualify for assumption approval
- You want a faster close (new loans can close in 21 days)
- You need to refinance the property immediately after closing

In Colorado right now, most FHA assumptions make sense. The rates locked in 2020 and 2021 are still dramatically lower than what lenders are offering in 2026.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't assume the lender will approve you without verifying income.** They will ask. Bring documentation early.

**Don't ignore the remaining loan term.** If there are only 15 years left on a 30-year original loan, you'll make higher payments. Make sure you're comfortable with that.

**Don't skip the home inspection.** Assuming a loan doesn't skip this. You still own the house. Inspect it.

**Don't wait until after offer acceptance to contact the lender.** Reach out before you write an offer. Know your approval odds first. This makes your offer stronger.

**Don't confuse "assumable" with "approved."** The loan is assumable. You still need approval.

## How to Make Your Offer Stand Out

If you're competing with other buyers for a home with an FHA assumption, here's what helps.

Offer proof of funds showing you have the down payment ready. Get pre-qualified for the assumption (contact the lender before offer). Include a short timeline in your offer. Sellers like knowing closing won't drag out.
