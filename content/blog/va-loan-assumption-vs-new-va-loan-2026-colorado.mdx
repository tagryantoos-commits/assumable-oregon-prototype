## The Setup: What We're Comparing

**Assumable VA loans:** Rates locked in from 2020 to 2023. So we're talking 2.25% to 3.5%.

Both options have zero down payment requirements. That's the VA loan superpower. But the rate difference? That's where the money lives.

## But Wait. There Are Catches.

I need to be straight with you. Assuming a VA loan isn't just "pick the rate and move in." There's paperwork. There's approval. And there's no guarantee the lender will sign off.

**The VA Loan Assumption Process:**
1. Find a property with an assumable VA loan (this is step one because not every home qualifies).
2. Get the seller to agree to let you assume their loan.
3. Submit paperwork to the VA and the lender proving you're a veteran and qualify.
4. The lender approves (or denies) the assumption.
5. Close and take over the payments.

The whole thing takes 30 to 60 days if everything lines up. If the lender says no? You're back to square one.

Here's the honest part: Some lenders make this easy. Some make it impossible. Some drag their feet so long you walk away. We've seen all three in Colorado.

And here's another thing. When you assume, you're taking over that exact loan. If it's a 27-year loan with 27 years left? You're paying for 27 years, not 30. Your timeline is locked in.

## So When Does Assuming Actually Win?

Assuming wins when:

1. **The rate gap is huge.** If someone locked in 2.4% and new loans are at 5.4%, the math is undeniable.

2. **You plan to stay long term.** If you're in the house for 10+ years, the monthly savings compound. If you're selling in 3 years, the benefit shrinks because you don't benefit from all those lower payments.

3. **The property actually has an assumable VA loan.** This is the sneaky part. Not every house for sale has this option. Inventory of assumable VA loans in Colorado is limited.

4. **The lender cooperates.** If you run into a lender that won't budge, a new loan becomes your only option.

## When a New VA Loan Makes Sense

A new VA loan is your move if:

1. **You can't find assumable properties.** If there's nothing on the market you want with an assumable VA loan, your choice is made for you.

2. **You need flexibility.** New loans come with standard terms. 15 years, 20 years, 30 years. You pick. Assumable loans lock you into whatever's left on the original note.

3. **The property has other issues.** Some sellers won't cooperate with assumptions. Some properties have liens or title problems. A new loan gives you a clean slate.

4. **You need a specific loan structure.** Cash-out refinance? That's not an assumption thing. That's a new loan conversation.
