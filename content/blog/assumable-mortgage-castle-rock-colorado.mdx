# Assumable Mortgage Castle Rock Colorado: Douglas County's Fastest-Growing City Has the Rates to Match

Castle Rock has been one of the fastest-growing cities in Colorado and the entire country. Located 30 miles south of Denver and 35 miles north of Colorado Springs on I-25, it's become the destination for buyers who want new construction, great schools, and room to breathe, without fully committing to either city's commute.

The growth has pushed home prices to $580,000–$780,000 for typical single-family homes, with newer construction pushing well above that. At 6.80% interest rates, even the entry-level Castle Rock market is expensive on a monthly basis.

What most Castle Rock buyers don't know: the city has excellent VA loan inventory from the 2020–2022 buying window, driven by military families commuting to Colorado Springs installations who chose Castle Rock for the lifestyle, schools, and Douglas County amenities.

## Why Castle Rock Has Strong VA Loan Inventory

Three Colorado Springs installations create VA loan inventory in Castle Rock:

**Peterson Space Force Base**, The largest employer on the south Colorado Springs base complex. Senior officers and NCOs frequently chose Castle Rock over the Springs itself, accepting a 35-mile commute for Douglas County schools and newer construction. Those buyers locked in VA loans at 2020–2022 rates and are now PCSing.

**Schriever Space Force Base**, Located east of Colorado Springs, Schriever's personnel base overlaps significantly with Peterson in terms of residential preferences. Castle Rock drew families from both installations.

**Fort Carson**, Some senior personnel and officers from Fort Carson chose to live north along the I-25 corridor. While Fort Carson families more commonly live in Colorado Springs proper or Fountain, Castle Rock captures a share of the higher-earning officer class.

The result: Castle Rock has meaningful VA loan inventory attached to homes in the $530,000–$700,000 range, right in the price tier where the monthly payment savings from an assumed VA loan are largest.

## Handling the Equity Gap

With Castle Rock's price range, equity gaps run $120,000–$180,000. That requires planning but is workable.

**Cash solution:** If you have $130,000–$160,000 liquid, you cover the gap outright. Monthly payment is just the assumed VA loan. At $1,107/month in savings on the first example, your payback period is about 9.8 years. Castle Rock is a long-term hold market, that return makes financial sense.

**Second mortgage:** Finance the $125,000 gap (the $635,000 home, assuming some negotiation) at 10% over 15 years, payment approximately $1,342/month. Combined with assumed first at $2,219/month: $3,561/month total. New single 6.80% loan on $635,000: $4,178/month. Save $617/month even with the second.

**Negotiate the PCS seller.** Military sellers on PCS orders need to close. They often have flexibility on price, especially if they're relocating to high-cost areas where BAH more than covers housing. A $20,000–$30,000 price reduction on a Castle Rock home is entirely realistic in a motivated sale.

Search Castle Rock and the surrounding Douglas County area (Franktown, Larkspur, nearby rural properties) for VA loan type with origination dates January 2020 – March 2022. Military sellers sometimes list in the late spring ahead of summer PCS orders, be ready to move.

**Step 2: Write an assumption-contingent offer that works for a PCS seller.**
Structure your offer around the seller's needs. Military sellers often have hard closing deadlines tied to their reporting date. Build that into your assumption contingency while managing servicer timeline expectations. A 75-day close aligned with a September 1 reporting date is a deal you can make work.

**Step 3: Process with the VA servicer.**
USAA, Navy Federal, Veterans United, and Lakeview hold significant Castle Rock VA volume. Each has a dedicated assumption team. Submit complete documentation, don't trickle in paperwork. A complete, organized submission shortens the review period.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is an assumable mortgage?
An assumable mortgage is an existing home loan that a buyer takes over from the seller at the original interest rate, balance, and terms. FHA, VA, and USDA loans are assumable. Conventional loans generally are not.

### How much can I save with an assumable mortgage?
On a $400,000 loan at 3% vs. 7%, you save $1,081 per month. That's $12,972 per year, and over $300,000 over the life of the loan. Real savings, not theoretical ones.

### Which loans are assumable?
FHA loans, VA loans, and USDA loans are all assumable. Conventional loans (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac) generally have due-on-sale clauses that prevent assumption. The most valuable assumable inventory comes from 2019-2022 originations.

### How do I find homes with assumable mortgages?
Most MLS listings don't flag assumable loans. You need to work with a specialist or use a service that tracks FHA and VA loan inventory. [Browse assumable homes in Colorado](/homes) to see what's available now.

### How long does the assumption process take?
Most assumptions close in 45-90 days. The main variable is the loan servicer's processing speed. Having all your documents ready upfront and working with an experienced assumption specialist helps.

### What is the equity gap?
The equity gap is the difference between the home's sale price and the existing loan balance. You cover this with cash, a second mortgage, or both. Even with a second mortgage, the blended rate often beats a new conventional loan.
