## Malmstrom Air Force Base and the Great Falls VA Market

Malmstrom Air Force Base sits seven miles northeast of Great Falls, the third-largest city in Montana with approximately 60,000 residents. The base is the home of the 341st Missile Wing — one of three wings in the country tasked with operating the Minuteman III ICBM fleet — and the 43rd Intelligence Squadron, the Air Force's primary signals intelligence unit.

The rotation cycle at Malmstrom runs three to four years for most missile combat crew members and support personnel. Missileer assignments involve a 36-month tour with limited flexibility for early departure. That predictability is good for sellers: these are service members who know exactly when they are leaving, who need to sell efficiently, and who often own homes financed with VA loans between 2020 and 2022.

Great Falls home prices are modest by Montana standards. The median single-family home in Cascade County sits in the $260,000 to $380,000 range, making Great Falls one of the most affordable VA markets in the northern Rocky Mountain region. Equity gaps on existing VA loans tend to run $20,000 to $75,000 — among the lowest in the state — which puts assumable transactions within reach for buyers who have saved a modest down payment or can qualify for a small gap loan.

**Great Falls VA Scenario:**

A buyer assuming a $285,000 VA loan at 2.875% pays **$1,182 per month** in principal and interest. A new $285,000 mortgage at 6.80% costs **$1,857 per month**. The buyer saves **$675 per month — $8,100 per year.**

For buyers coming from larger markets like Denver, Phoenix, or the Puget Sound area, Great Falls offers a compelling combination: low equity gaps, strong monthly savings on the assumption, and access to a community tightly connected to Malmstrom's mission. The area draws military retirees and DoD civilians who want to stay close to the base long-term.

**What to know about Malmstrom PCS sellers:**

Most missile crew members at Malmstrom are junior officers, typically 1st and 2nd Lieutenants, who bought modest homes in the $250,000 to $330,000 range. Sellers in this market are often motivated. PCS orders give them a specific departure window. DoD relocation assistance covers some closing costs. These are not sellers who are testing the market — they have a mission to complete and a timeline to keep.

## Gap Loan Math: Does Bozeman Still Make Sense?

The most common question about Bozeman assumptions is whether the high equity gap makes the deal worthwhile. Here is the actual math on a high-gap Bozeman scenario.

A buyer assumes a $580,000 VA loan at 2.75% on a home currently worth $710,000. The equity gap is $130,000. The buyer structures a 15-year gap loan at 8.5%:

- **Assumed portion:** $580,000 at 2.75% = **$2,368/mo** (P&I)
- **Gap loan:** $130,000 at 8.5% for 15 years = **$1,281/mo** (P&I)
- **Combined monthly payment:** $3,649/mo

**Versus a new 30-year conventional mortgage on the same $710,000 property at 6.80%:**
$710,000 at 6.80% = **$4,626/mo** (P&I)

The assumption buyer with the gap loan saves **$977 per month** compared to a buyer who finances conventionally. Over 15 years — the life of the gap loan — total savings exceed $175,000. After year 15, when the gap loan is paid off, the assumption buyer's payment drops to $2,368/mo while a conventional buyer's payment is still $4,626/mo. The gap between those two payments grows larger over time, not smaller.

The gap loan adds complexity. It adds a second monthly payment. But it does not eliminate the financial logic of the assumption in high-value markets like Bozeman. The math works because the rate differential is so extreme — 2.75% versus 6.80% is not a marginal difference. It is a structural advantage that compounds over the life of the loan.

## Montana Closing Process: What Buyers Need to Know

Montana is a **deed of trust state** with title company closings. Real estate attorneys are not required in Montana, which keeps closing costs lower than attorney-state markets like Massachusetts or New York. Title companies handle escrow, title search, and closing documentation.

Key Montana-specific notes for assumable transactions:

**No transfer tax on assumed loan balance.** Montana does not impose a real estate transfer tax on the assumed loan balance. The buyer pays title insurance, recording fees, and assumption processing fees, but not a transfer tax. This is a meaningful cost advantage versus states that charge transfer tax on the full assumed balance.

**Assumption processing timeline:** 45 to 75 days from accepted offer to close is standard for Montana assumable transactions. FHA assumptions (HUD/servicer approval required) tend to run 60-75 days. VA assumptions where the lender handles approval in-house can sometimes close faster, particularly with large servicers like USAA, Navy Federal, or Veterans United that have established VA assumption departments.

**Closing costs for the assumption buyer** typically include: assumption processing fee ($300-$900 depending on servicer), title insurance ($600-$1,200 depending on purchase price), recording fees ($50-$150), and any lender fees associated with a gap loan if applicable. Montana closing costs are generally lower than coastal markets.

**Title company selection:** In Montana, the listing agent or buyer's agent often recommends a local title company with prior assumption experience. This matters. Title companies that have not processed VA or FHA assumption transactions move slowly and make documentation errors. Ask specifically for a company that has closed at least 10-15 assumption transactions in the past 24 months.

## Non-Veteran VA Assumption Eligibility in Montana

One of the most valuable and most misunderstood aspects of VA loan assumptions: you do not need to be a veteran to assume a VA loan. Any buyer who meets the creditworthiness standards of the VA assumption process can assume a VA loan from a qualifying seller — even if they have never served in the military.

This applies to every VA loan in the Malmstrom AFB corridor, Bozeman's military officer market, and any other Montana VA loan from the 2020-2022 buying window.

The process for non-veteran buyers assuming VA loans:

1. Identify a property with a VA loan (MLS notes, VA loan in public records, or seller disclosure)
2. Negotiate assumption terms with the seller
3. Submit a credit application to the loan servicer — the same lender who originated the VA loan
4. Servicer reviews credit score (typically 620+ minimum), debt-to-income ratio, and income documentation
5. VA issues a credit approval for the assumption
6. Close with a Montana title company

The servicer's credit standards for non-veteran VA assumption approvals tend to be slightly more conservative than VA purchase loan standards. Expect to need a 620-660+ credit score, documented income, and a DTI below 45%. These are not extraordinary standards — most qualified buyers will clear them.

**Important for VA sellers:** If a non-veteran assumes a VA loan without what is called VA entitlement substitution, the seller's VA entitlement remains tied to the property until the loan is paid off. This can prevent the veteran seller from using their full VA entitlement to buy their next home. Most VA lenders can advise on whether substitution is required for the seller's specific situation. In practice, many Malmstrom and Bozeman sellers have already paid down enough of their loan that this does not affect their future VA borrowing capacity, but it is worth addressing early in the transaction.

## How to Find Assumable Properties in Montana

Most assumable properties in Montana are not marketed as assumable. Sellers and their listing agents do not typically know to highlight this feature, and Montana MLS systems do not have a dedicated assumable mortgage filter in most cases.

Effective search strategies:

**Search MLS for loan type language.** In many Montana MLS instances, the listing agent enters financing type as FHA or VA. Filtering by these loan types narrows the field to potentially assumable properties.

**Public records search.** Cascade County, Gallatin County, Missoula County, and Yellowstone County each maintain public deed and mortgage records. A search for FHA and VA loans originated between January 2020 and December 2022 will identify properties with assumable loan structures.

**Ask directly.** When a property is listed with a VA or FHA loan, simply ask the listing agent: "Is the seller open to an assumption?" Many sellers who have never considered this as an option respond positively once the math is explained. A seller who can command a higher price because of the assumption benefit has a direct financial incentive to cooperate.

**Work with a specialist.** Agents who understand the assumption process can run a targeted MLS search, verify loan assumability before writing an offer, and draft an offer that addresses the servicer approval contingency properly. This is the most efficient path.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Montana Assumable Mortgages

**Can I assume a mortgage in Montana without a real estate agent?**
Technically, a buyer can negotiate an assumption directly. Practically, most servicers require both parties to work through licensed agents or have legal representation for the assumption documentation. The transaction complexity — credit approval, servicer coordination, title search, and escrow — makes agent representation worth the commission.

**How long does a VA assumption take in Montana?**
Plan for 45-65 days from accepted offer to close for a VA assumption in Montana. The longest variable is servicer processing time. USAA and Navy Federal typically process faster than smaller lenders. Budget 60 days conservatively when scheduling a seller's departure timeline.

**What credit score do I need to assume an FHA loan in Montana?**
FHA assumption credit minimums are set by HUD. As of 2026, HUD requires a minimum 620 credit score for creditworthy buyer assumption approval. Some servicers apply more conservative overlays — 640 is a common practical floor. Debt-to-income ratio below 43-45% and documented income are also required.

**Is the equity gap tax deductible?**
The assumed mortgage interest is deductible exactly like standard mortgage interest. If you also take a gap loan, the gap loan interest is deductible as well (on a primary residence, subject to standard IRS mortgage interest deduction limits). Consult a tax advisor for specifics.

**Can I assume a mortgage on a Bozeman rental property?**
FHA loans are assumable only by owner-occupants. VA loans can be assumed by non-veteran non-occupants in some circumstances, though servicer policies vary. If you are buying an investment property, the assumption path is more limited and requires direct servicer consultation.
