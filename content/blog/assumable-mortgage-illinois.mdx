## Why Illinois Has So Many Assumable Loans

### Four Military Installations Creating VA Inventory

Illinois is not a state that dominates military headlines, but it holds a surprisingly deep military presence spread across three distinct regional markets. Each installation created VA loan buying during the 2019-2022 rate window, and each now produces PCS-cycle inventory.

Scott AFB sits in St. Clair County, directly across the Mississippi River from St. Louis in the communities of O'Fallon, Shiloh, Lebanon, Mascoutah, and Belleville. The installation is home to two of the most powerful commands in the Air Force: US Transportation Command (TRANSCOM) and Air Mobility Command (AMC). These are not frontline combat units — they are headquarters commands, which means Scott AFB has a distinctly different demographic than combat bases.

TRANSCOM manages all US military logistics globally. AMC moves people and cargo by air for every branch of the armed forces. Both commands require officers and senior NCOs — experienced, well-compensated military buyers who purchased homes in the $220,000 to $350,000 range during the rate window. With approximately 15,000 active-duty personnel and DoD civilian employees, Scott AFB generated substantial VA loan volume from 2019 through 2022 in communities within a 20-mile radius.

The critical dynamic at Scott: TRANSCOM and AMC both operate on traditional PCS rotation schedules, typically 2 to 4 years. As those rotations conclude, those 2020 and 2021 VA loans cycle back to market. O'Fallon and Shiloh see the most volume — affordable prices, excellent schools, and proximity to the main gate.

**Naval Station Great Lakes — North Chicago, Illinois**

Naval Station Great Lakes is the largest Navy installation in the world by training throughput. Every Navy recruit in the United States goes through boot camp here. Beyond the recruit training command, the installation houses multiple "A" and "C" school technical training pipelines, the Navy's nuclear power school program, and support commands with permanent party personnel.

The permanent party component — estimated at 7,000 to 9,000 active-duty sailors and their families — created steady FHA and VA purchase activity in the North Shore communities: North Chicago, Waukegan, Gurnee, Round Lake, Libertyville, and Zion. The ongoing training mission means Naval Station Great Lakes maintains permanent party turnover constantly. Instructors complete 2-3 year shore duty tours. Senior enlisted leaders rotate in and out. Every rotation cycle produces assumable inventory in Lake County.

**Rock Island Arsenal — Rock Island, Illinois (Quad Cities)**

Rock Island Arsenal sits on an island in the Mississippi River, split between Rock Island, Illinois, and Davenport, Iowa. The Arsenal is a Joint Manufacturing and Technology Center — the US military's largest government-owned weapons manufacturing facility — employing approximately 6,000 active-duty personnel, DoD civilians, and contractors.

The Quad Cities market (Rock Island, Moline on the Illinois side; Davenport, Bettendorf on the Iowa side) is one of the most affordable metro areas in the Midwest. ## Who Can Assume a Mortgage in Illinois

### FHA Loans: Open to Anyone

Every FHA loan — regardless of when it was originated — is assumable by any buyer who can qualify with the lender. You do not need to be a veteran, active duty, or have any prior military affiliation. FHA assumptions are available to first-time buyers, move-up buyers, investors (with restrictions), and civilian buyers of every background.

The lender must approve the new borrower. They will verify income, credit, and debt-to-income ratio using standard FHA qualifying guidelines. The loan package is submitted to the lender's assumption department — not a standard underwriting team — which means the process takes 30 to 60 days rather than the typical 21 to 30 days for a conventional purchase.

FHA loans in Illinois's Chicago suburbs are the primary source of civilian assumable inventory. Hundreds of thousands of FHA loans were originated in Cook, DuPage, Kane, Will, and Lake counties during the rate window.

### VA Loans: Veterans and Non-Veterans Both Qualify

VA loans are assumable, but the rules depend on who is doing the assuming.

**Veteran or active-duty buyer assuming a VA loan:** The cleanest scenario. The assuming buyer substitutes their VA entitlement for the seller's. The seller's entitlement is fully restored. Both parties emerge without complication, and the assuming buyer does not need to use their own VA loan eligibility for the purchase. They step into the seller's loan using a process called entitlement substitution.

**Non-veteran buyer assuming a VA loan:** This is legal and happens regularly — but requires a critical decision by the seller. When a non-veteran assumes a VA loan, the selling veteran's VA entitlement remains tied to that property until the loan is paid off. This means the veteran cannot use their full VA entitlement for another purchase until either the assuming buyer refinances out of the VA loan or pays it off entirely.

Most veterans selling in the Scott AFB and Naval Station Great Lakes markets are either PCSing to another duty station (where they will use VA again) or separating from service. Sellers PCSing who want to protect their VA entitlement typically prefer veteran buyers — but non-veteran buyers are not categorically excluded. Each situation requires a direct conversation with the seller and their lender.

### The Equity Gap: What Illinois Buyers Need to Have Ready

The equity gap is the difference between the home's current value and the remaining loan balance. In Illinois, equity gaps are typically smaller than in coastal or high-appreciation markets. Will County gaps often run $50,000 to $80,000. Rock Island Arsenal gaps can be $30,000 to $60,000. DuPage County gaps are larger but manageable for the buyer profiles those markets attract.

Options for covering the gap:

1. **Cash at closing:** Simplest. Bring the gap as a lump sum.
2. **Second mortgage / gap loan:** Borrow the gap amount at a separate rate, typically 7% to 9%. Even with gap loan payments, the combined payment on assumed first + gap second is usually lower than a new conventional purchase loan at current rates.
3. **Seller concession:** In softer markets, sellers sometimes reduce the purchase price to lower the effective gap.
4. **Family gift:** FHA allows gift funds to cover the gap from family members. VA has similar provisions.

The key calculation: what does the assumed first mortgage payment plus a gap loan payment equal? In the majority of Illinois scenarios at current prices, that combined payment still beats a new 6.75% loan on the full purchase price by $200 to $600 per month.

## The Assumption Process in Illinois: Timeline and What to Expect

### Step 1: Identify the Property and Verify the Loan

Once you find a property with an assumable FHA or VA loan, confirm the key details before making an offer:

- Current loan balance (ask seller or listing agent, or request payoff statement)
- Interest rate on the existing note
- Lender or servicer handling the loan
- Remaining term

### Step 2: Write the Purchase Contract

Your contract should explicitly include assumption language. The offer price typically equals the home's appraised value. The assumption is the financing mechanism — not a discount. Sellers do not "give away" the low rate; buyers pay full market value and gain the right to assume the loan.

Illinois real estate contracts can accommodate assumption transactions — the concept is legally straightforward. An agent familiar with assumptions should handle the language to avoid ambiguities around the equity gap, timeline, and seller entitlement (for VA loans).

### Step 3: Submit to the Lender's Assumption Department

Every FHA and VA lender has an assumption processing team. This is separate from the origination or underwriting team. The submission package typically includes:

- Buyer's credit report authorization
- Buyer's income documentation (W-2s, pay stubs, tax returns)
- Signed purchase contract
- Assumption application (lender-specific form)

Processing time runs 30 to 60 days for FHA assumptions. VA assumptions average 45 to 75 days. Build this into the purchase contract timeline — do not use a standard 30-day close expectation.

### Step 4: Title and Closing

Illinois is an attorney state for real estate closings. A closing attorney handles the title transfer, assumption deed, and closing documents. Lender assumption departments typically work directly with closing attorneys in Illinois, which streamlines the coordination compared to states that use title companies exclusively.

Closing costs on an assumption are lower than a new purchase loan because there is no origination fee and no new appraisal required by the lender (in most cases). Budget $2,000 to $3,500 in total closing costs versus $5,000 to $10,000 on a new loan.

### Common Illinois-Specific Considerations

**Property taxes:** Illinois has some of the highest property taxes in the country. Especially in Cook County, factor in the full property tax picture when calculating monthly payment comparisons. An assumed loan with low rates still carries the same tax burden as any other purchase.

**Scott AFB / Metro East dual-state awareness:** The St. Louis metropolitan area straddles Illinois and Missouri. Homes in Missouri (O'Fallon MO, St. Peters, St. Charles) are not covered by the same Illinois closing attorney requirement and may involve different processes. Make sure your team knows which side of the river they are working on.

**Rock Island Arsenal / Quad Cities cross-state market:** Similarly, homes on the Iowa side of the Quad Cities (Davenport, Bettendorf) operate under Iowa real estate law. If you are searching the Rock Island Arsenal market, clarify whether you are focused on the Illinois communities (Rock Island, Moline, East Moline) or the Iowa communities across the river.

## Frequently Asked Questions: Assumable Mortgages in Illinois

**Can a civilian buyer assume a VA loan in Illinois?**

Yes. Civilian buyers can assume VA loans. When a non-veteran assumes a VA loan, the selling veteran's entitlement stays tied to the property until the loan is paid off. Many VA sellers prefer veteran buyers specifically to protect their entitlement. However, non-veteran assumptions happen regularly — particularly when the seller has already used their entitlement on another property or is willing to trade a clean entitlement for a faster, smoother sale.

**How long does an assumption take in Illinois?**

FHA assumptions run 30 to 60 days from submission to close. VA assumptions average 45 to 75 days. Illinois's attorney closing process adds coordination steps that some other states do not have, but closing attorneys in the Scott AFB and Chicago markets handle these routinely. Build the extended timeline into your purchase contract from the start.

**What credit score do I need to assume an FHA or VA loan in Illinois?**

FHA assumption qualification follows standard FHA guidelines — a 580 credit score minimum with 3.5% down (or 500-579 with 10% down). VA assumption qualification uses the individual lender's standards. In practice, most lenders want to see 620+ for VA assumptions. Strong debt-to-income ratios matter as much as credit score.

**Are Illinois home prices high enough to make the savings worthwhile?**

Yes — particularly in Chicago suburbs. On a Naperville FHA loan at 3.25% with a $370,000 balance, monthly savings versus refinancing at 6.75% exceed $780. Even in more affordable markets like Will County or the Quad Cities, savings of $400 to $560 per month are typical. Over a 10-year holding period, that is $48,000 to $94,000 in interest that stays in your pocket instead of going to the lender.

**What is the equity gap in Illinois assumable transactions?**

The equity gap is the difference between the home's current appraised value and the remaining loan balance. In Illinois, equity gaps are generally smaller than coastal markets: $40,000 to $80,000 in most Chicago suburban markets, $30,000 to $65,000 in the Quad Cities, $55,000 to $95,000 in the Scott AFB corridor. Larger DuPage County homes may have $100,000 to $170,000 gaps. Buyers cover the gap with cash, a second mortgage, or seller concessions.

**Does Illinois require an attorney for assumption closings?**

Illinois is an attorney state for real estate transactions. A closing attorney handles the transfer documents, assumption deed, and closing process. This is standard in Illinois regardless of whether the transaction is conventional, FHA, VA, or assumable. Your real estate agent and lender assumption team will coordinate with the closing attorney.
