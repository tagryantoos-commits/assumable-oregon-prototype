# Assumable Mortgage Florida: The Complete Guide

Florida is one of the top five assumable mortgage states in the country.

That's not a marketing claim. It's the logical result of three overlapping facts: Florida has three major military installations (NAS Jacksonville, MacDill AFB, NAS Pensacola), it was one of the most active FHA purchase states during the 2020-2022 rate window, and it had massive real estate appreciation that now makes the gap between old locked rates and current market rates extremely valuable.

The buyers who know this are finding homes at $500-700 per month less than anyone financing through a new conventional loan. The buyers who don't know this are paying full rate on properties that have assumable paper sitting right there in the deed records.

This guide covers how assumable mortgages work in Florida, which markets have the most inventory, who can qualify, and what the actual numbers look like.

## Why Florida Has So Many Assumable Loans

### Military Concentration

Florida's three major installations generated continuous VA loan originations throughout 2019-2022:

**NAS Jacksonville** — One of the Navy's largest shore commands. Hosts the Atlantic Fleet's patrol and reconnaissance aircraft, plus several carrier air wings. The Jacksonville metro, including Orange Park, Fleming Island, and Clay County, has deep VA loan penetration from sailors and officers who bought during the low-rate window.

**MacDill Air Force Base** — Home to CENTCOM and SOCOM. Located inside Tampa, MacDill's tenant commands rotate servicemembers constantly. The Tampa Bay area—Riverview, Brandon, Apollo Beach, Valrico, Wesley Chapel—has significant VA inventory from military buyers who closed in 2020 and 2021 and are now PCSing.

**NAS Pensacola** — The Cradle of Naval Aviation. Student naval aviators rotate through faster than almost any other installation, creating a steady pipeline of VA sellers who bought recently and are now departing. The Pensacola metro, including Gulf Breeze, Pace, and Cantonment, consistently generates new assumable listings.

Between these three installations and their surrounding communities, Florida produces more VA loan originations per year than most states.

### The FHA Factor

Florida's assumable market isn't limited to military buyers. During 2020-2022, Florida was one of the most active states for FHA purchase originations nationally. First-time buyers in Orlando, Miami, Fort Lauderdale, West Palm Beach, and Gainesville locked in FHA loans at rates between 2.75% and 3.5%.

Those loans are fully assumable. Any buyer who qualifies through the lender can take one over—no military service required, no veteran status required.

This means Florida's assumable inventory is broader and deeper than a purely military-focused search would show.

## Who Can Assume a Florida Mortgage?

This is the question people get wrong most often. They assume (correctly) that veterans can assume VA loans, and then they stop there.

Here's the full picture:

**VA Loans — Assumable by Anyone**
VA loans are assumable by any buyer who meets the lender's credit and income requirements. You do not need to be a veteran. You do not need military service. You need a decent credit score, verifiable income, and the ability to qualify through the servicer.

The catch for sellers: if a non-veteran assumes the VA loan, the seller's VA entitlement stays tied to that property until the loan is paid off. Some sellers are sensitive to this. Others don't care, especially if they've already used their entitlement for a new purchase. A veteran buyer who substitutes their own entitlement is the cleanest outcome for the seller.

**FHA Loans — Assumable by Anyone Who Qualifies**
FHA assumptions have no service-related restriction whatsoever. Qualify through the lender, get approval from HUD, close. It's the same qualification process as a new FHA loan, but you're stepping into the existing rate instead of being quoted today's rate.

**USDA Loans — Assumable with Lender Approval**
Florida's rural areas—particularly in the northern counties (Alachua, Marion, Columbia) and along the Nature Coast—have USDA loan inventory. USDA assumptions require lender approval and the buyer must meet USDA income limits, but the process exists and is underused.

## The Equity Gap in Florida

Florida's appreciation since 2020 has created meaningful equity gaps in most markets. Here's how to think through it:

A seller who bought at $280,000 in 2020 is now listing at $395,000. Their remaining loan balance might be $258,000. The gap is $137,000—the difference between what they owe and what they're selling for.

As a buyer, you need to cover that gap. Your options:

1. **Cash:** Pay the gap at closing. This is the cleanest path but requires liquidity.
2. **Second mortgage:** Borrow the gap from a lender that works with assumption buyers. Rates on the second are at market, but the blended rate (first + second) is often still well below what you'd pay on a full new mortgage.
3. **Negotiated price:** Some sellers will price their home lower specifically to attract an assumable buyer, because they know the assumable buyer pool is strong and motivated.

The math on a blended rate is almost always favorable. Assume $258,000 at 2.875%, finance $137,000 at 6.75%, and your blended rate is approximately 4.1%—still well below what any lender will quote you today.

## The Florida Process: What to Expect

Florida is a **mortgage state** (not a deed of trust state), which means the legal mechanics at closing are slightly different from states like Colorado or Texas. This doesn't materially affect how mortgage assumptions work—the process plays out at the servicer level regardless of state law.

**Timeline:** 45-90 days from offer to close. Servicers are the bottleneck. Some move at 45 days with proper documentation and consistent follow-up. Others drag toward 90. VA assumptions through lenders like USAA, Navy Federal, and Pentagon Federal tend to move faster because they're experienced with the process. Banks that rarely handle assumptions slow down considerably.

**What you'll need:**
- Credit authorization for the servicer to pull your file
- Proof of income (last 2 years tax returns, 60 days pay stubs)
- Asset statements
- Signed purchase agreement with assumption contingency
- Assumption application (servicer-specific)

Florida's property insurance costs are relevant to your total monthly payment calculation. Get insurance quotes before you're under contract, not after. The mortgage savings from an assumption are typically large enough that the insurance math still favors the assumable path, but run the full number.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I find assumable mortgages in Florida?
Search MLS listings that mention "assumable" in the remarks, filter for FHA and VA purchases from 2020-2022, or work with an agent who tracks assumable inventory. Neighborhoods near NAS Jacksonville, MacDill AFB, and NAS Pensacola have the highest concentration of VA assumable listings.

### Can a non-veteran assume a VA loan in Florida?
Yes. VA loans are assumable by any buyer who meets the lender's credit and income qualification standards. Military service is not required to assume. The seller's VA entitlement stays with the property until the loan is paid off, which some sellers prefer to avoid by working with a veteran buyer who can substitute their own entitlement.

### How long does a mortgage assumption take in Florida?
Plan for 45-90 days from offer to close. Florida's mortgage-state legal structure doesn't create additional delays, but servicers vary widely in speed. VA assumptions through military-focused lenders (USAA, Navy Federal) tend to close faster than conventional bank servicers.

### What credit score do I need to assume a mortgage in Florida?
The servicer sets the standard, not a federal rule. For VA assumptions, 620 is generally the floor. For FHA, HUD allows down to 580 with a 3.5% down payment on new loans, and servicers apply similar standards to assumptions. Individual lenders may require higher scores. The better your credit, the smoother the process.

### What is the equity gap on assumable Florida homes?
Florida's appreciation since 2020 means most sellers have substantial equity. The gap between remaining loan balance and sale price is the amount you need to cover in cash or with a second mortgage. In Jacksonville and Pensacola, this is often $50,000-$150,000. In Tampa and South Florida, gaps can run higher. The key metric is the blended rate after combining the assumed first and any second mortgage—it should still be well below current market rates.

### Are there assumable FHA loans in Florida?
Yes. Florida had high FHA purchase volume in 2020-2022, particularly in Orlando, Jacksonville, Miami-Dade, and Broward counties. FHA loans from that window are assumable by any qualified buyer, with no military service requirement. Check homes that sold as FHA purchases in that period.

### Can I assume a mortgage and rent it out in Florida?
VA loans have an owner-occupancy requirement—you must intend to occupy the property as your primary residence. You can't assume a VA loan specifically to rent it out immediately. FHA loans have the same owner-occupancy requirement. However, you can occupy the property and later move without immediately paying off the loan. Many buyers do this legitimately—live in the property, then convert to rental after occupancy requirements are satisfied. USDA loans have similar occupancy rules.

### Which Florida city has the most assumable inventory?
Jacksonville typically leads because of its dual military installation footprint (NAS Jacksonville and Naval Station Mayport). Tampa Bay is competitive because MacDill generates high-grade VA loans. Pensacola is the most concentrated by percentage of military population. All three are worth searching.
